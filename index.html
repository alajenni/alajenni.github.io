About

I'm a Computer Science and Philosophy student at UC Santa Cruz (graduating June 2026), pursuing APM and TPM internships where I can bridge technical teams with business stakeholders to build meaningful products.

My path has been anything but linear — and I think that's a feature, not a bug. I've coordinated large-scale enterprise conferences at Moscone Center, managed customer relationships at Bank of America, and explored consumer product thinking through a BeReal PM externship.

What ties it all together is a consistent thread: end-to-end execution. Whether I'm wrangling logistics for a 50,000-person event, gathering requirements from stakeholders, or synthesizing user insights into product decisions — I care about making things actually work.

Outside of work and school, I'm drawn to questions about consciousness, memory, and identity — the same curiosity that makes me think hard about why people use products the way they do.
